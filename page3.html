QEESI

Quick Environmental Exposure and Sensitivity Inventory

The QEESI Team is looking for data on how to continue the transition of the QEESI to an online questionnaire that assists potential patients in the discovery, tracking, and treatment of chemical intolerances.

Project Focus:

Language

Assess the ability of QEESI to communicate clearly with all users.

Standards

Adhere to standards in accessibility, security, and responsiveness.

Efficiency

Promote efficiency and accuracy of sensitivity identification.

Project Timeline

Distribution of Work

WIth a team of five it was important that we delegate tasks quickly, efficienctly, and with the intention of providing everyone an opportunity to participate and learn. We partnered up across three phases: Heuristic Evaluation, Competitive Analysis, and Interview Sessions.

Considering my experience, I suggested the others take the reins on the individual assignments and offered my guidance as we progresses through the research.

My personal responsibilities included creating the project plan, project schedule, interview scheduling, moderating an interview, and creation of the research report that would accompany our presentation.

Heuristic Evaluation

Wei and Eric completed the Heuristic Evaluation based on the Usability Heuristics popularized by Jakob Nielsen

Competitive Analysis

Peidi and Kramer completed the Competitive Analysis which was focused on direct competitiors (WebMD, Mayo Clinic, and Psychology Today) as well as indirect competitors (Google Forms, 16 Personalities, and Qualtrics). The goal was to focus on companies delivering health data and companies delivering quality questionnaires with a focus on usability.

Live Interviews

The group then developed a screener that included demographic data for the purpose of exploritative research as well as questions surrounding possible medical experience, prior experiences with the QEESI, and questions from the BREESI (a screener currently used for the QEESI). 

Seven interviews were scheduled and conducted by pairs of researchers (one moderator and one note taker). Prior to scheduling, Kramer and Eric teamed up to draft up a script which was later evalutated and finalized as a team.

The exercises included tasks associated with completing the QEESI including the interpretation of a bar or spider chart which varied by participant to allow quantitative analysis on which was more user friendly. Finally a SUS test was completed.

Report Presentation

To finalize our research, Wei drafted a presentation mockup in Figma while Kramer, Peidi, and myself sketched out solutions for the issues that we discovered during our research. Eric spent his time finalizing drafts of the new results page. All of these can be found in our finalized presentation. I drafted a more formal report to assist the QEESI team moving forward considering their access to the research team would be limited after the conclusion of our semester.
