Hello, I am Ken Copelin

I'm an aspiring User Researcher in Austin, Texas

Currently I'm conducting user research at the University of Texas in Austin focusing on the fields of education and healthcare. My graduate research has conducting interviews prisoners and patients in an effort to better our future. 

Below you'll find some more details about my research as well as the techniques I've used and the wonderful people I've had the opportunity to work with here at The University of Texas.

I've always thought of myself as a problem solver. Growing up, I spent my afternoons working on cars with my father and my evenings using my computer to team up with people around the world in various video games. After graduation I served as a Combat Medic in the Army where I learned to observe, listen, and guide people on a path to a brighter and healthier future. My undegraduate studies inverted that experience. I was able to spend time on myself and discover where I was supposed to end up. After many majors, I found what I wanted to do. Now as a graduate student at the University of Texas, I'm excited to say that I'm spending long hours working with wonderful people as I learn to transition my experience to an industry where people matter most.

As I learned in the military, tools will change over time. We learn to use them and we often disgard them when the next best thing comes to light. While people may come and go the challenges we each face are often relevant. People are my focus. I spend time trying to better my mastery of software like Sketch and the tools of our trade such as user testing, card sorts, and the like. But my focus will always be the needs of the people I'm serving. As a User Researcher, balancing the needs of my users with the needs of the business is my end-goal.
